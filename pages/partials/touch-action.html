Touch-Action

The touch-action property in CSS gives you control over the effect of touchscreen interactions with an element, similar to the more widely-used pointer-events property used to control mouse interactions.

HTML

<div class="overflow-auto width-300px height-400px touch-action-auto">
  <img src='https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1526778548025-fa2f459cd5c1?ixlib=rb-1.2.1&q=85&fm=jpg&crop=entropy&cs=srgb&ixid=eyJhcHBfaWQiOjE0NTg5fQ' alt=''>
</div>

SCSS

$touch-action: ();
$touch-action: map-merge(
  (
    "auto": auto,
    "none": none,
    "pan-x": pan-x,
    "pan-y": pan-y,
    "manipulation": manipulation,
    "pan-left": pan-left,
    "pan-right": pan-right,
    "pan-down": pan-down,
    "pan-up": pan-up,
    "pinch-zoom": pinch-zoom,
  ),
  $touch-action
);
